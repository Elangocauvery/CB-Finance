🏭 Industry Backdrop — Q1 FY27 (Apr–Jun 2026)
Industry QAAUM
India's mutual fund industry quarterly-average AUM reached ₹83.28 lakh Cr, up 15.4% YoY and 2.0% QoQ. Equity schemes held a steady 54% share (~₹45.20 lakh Cr). Both ICICI Pru AMC and HDFC AMC grew QAAUM more slowly than this industry pace in percentage-point-share terms this quarter, ceding a few bps of market share even as absolute AUM rose.
Equity net sales momentum
Quarterly net sales into equity schemes were ₹1,13,834 Cr, down slightly from ₹1,23,898 Cr in Q4 FY26 but still robust — a favourable backdrop that helped both companies post double-digit fee-revenue growth despite a soft March 2026 base quarter.
Employee-cost inflation, sector-wide
Both companies flagged higher employee costs as the swing factor behind sequential operating-profit softness — ICICI Pru AMC's employee expense rose 11% YoY (ESOP-led), HDFC AMC's rose 31% YoY. This looks like a genuine sector-wide FY27 wage/ESOP reset rather than a company-specific issue.
TER regulation — a non-event so far
Both managements explicitly called out zero-to-minimal P&L impact from SEBI's total-expense-ratio (TER) relook this quarter — a relief given this was flagged as the single biggest regulatory overhang heading into FY27.
🎯 CB Research one-line take: Both AMCs delivered genuinely strong operating-revenue growth in Q1 FY27, but a meaningful share of the PAT beat in both cases came from "other income" (treasury/investment-book gains) rather than the core fee business — investors should weight operating profit ex-OI more heavily than headline PAT when judging quality of these results. Markets agreed with this reading: both stocks fell in the days after results (ICICI Pru AMC ~4%, HDFC AMC ~5%) even as most brokerages retained positive ratings. 🎯 CB Research முடிவு: இரு நிறுவனங்களும் இயக்க வருவாயில் வலுவான வளர்ச்சியைக் காட்டினாலும், PAT-யின் ஒரு பகுதி "மற்ற வருமானம்" (treasury gains) மூலமே வந்துள்ளது.

HDFC AMCHDFC AMC's PAT grew 34.5% QoQ, its best sequential jump in years — but its own "operating profit ex-OI" line actually fell 2.1% QoQ. How should an investor reconcile these two facts?
A. The PAT growth is the real story — ignore the operating-profit line.
B. Almost all the QoQ PAT jump came from "other income" rebounding off an unusually low Q4 FY26 base (₹11.6 Cr → ₹262.8 Cr), not from the core fee business, which actually softened slightly.
C. It signals HDFC AMC is diversifying away from its core AMC business into treasury income.
CB's view: Answer B. Other income moved from a very low ₹11.6 Cr base in Q4 FY26 to ₹262.8 Cr in Q1 FY27 — a swing of ~₹251 Cr, which alone explains most of the ~₹214 Cr QoQ PAT increase.
