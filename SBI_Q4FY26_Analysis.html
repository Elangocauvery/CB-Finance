Business Analysis
Deep-dive: Strengths, risks, and outlook for SBI in FY27

📈 Profit & Revenue Quality

Net Profit of ₹19,684 Cr in Q4 FY26 — up +5.6% YoY — marks four consecutive quarters of profit growth. FY26 full-year PAT of ₹80,032 Cr is the bank's all-time record, growing +12.88% YoY.

NII grew +4.1% YoY to ₹44,380 Cr, driven by a +16.9% expansion in advances. However, interest income quality slightly declined as treasury income fell ₹1,471 Cr due to rising G-Sec yields — a transient headwind.

Operating profit fell –11.4% YoY in Q4, primarily from lower non-interest income and higher OpEx. This is the key miss vs street expectations.

🏦 Asset Quality — Structural Improvement

GNPA declined to 1.49% from 1.82% a year ago — a two-decade low. NNPA stands at just 0.39%. Slippage ratio for FY26 came in at only 0.54%, reflecting disciplined underwriting.

Provision Coverage Ratio (PCR) stands at 74.36%, with additional non-NPA provisions of ₹29,713 Cr (~158% of Net NPA) acting as a substantial buffer. Credit cost at 0.37% is among the lowest in recent history.

International GNPA at 0.13% is a multi-year low, underscoring high-quality offshore book composition.

⚠️ NIM Pressure — Key Risk

Domestic NIM compressed to 2.93% in Q4 FY26, down 21 bps YoY and 18 bps QoQ. Whole-bank NIM at 2.81% for the quarter vs 2.99% in Q4 FY25. This drove the 7% stock correction on results day.

The compression stems from two forces: RBI rate cuts repricing floating loans faster than term deposits mature, and rising G-Sec yields causing treasury mark-to-market losses. FY26 full-year domestic NIM at 3.03%.

Management guidance: domestic NIM ~3% in FY27 — implying stabilisation rather than recovery. Rate transmission lag on deposit repricing may provide marginal tailwind in H2 FY27.

💰 Balance Sheet & Capital Strength

Total Business crossed ₹109 Trillion. Deposits at ₹59.8 lakh crore (+11% YoY); Advances at ₹49.3 lakh crore (+16.9% YoY). Domestic C/D ratio at 73.08%, leaving ample room for credit deployment.

CRAR strengthened to 15.40% (from 14.25%) and CET-1 rose to 12.29% (from 10.81%) — signalling strong capital self-sufficiency. SBI can fund ~₹12 trillion additional credit growth without equity dilution. Pending SBI AMC IPO will further boost CET-1.

LCR at ~124% well above the 100% regulatory floor; new RBI LCR guidelines expected to add 3–4% improvement.

🌱 Loan Growth — Diversified Engine

RAM (Retail, Agri, MSME) advances grew +17.1% YoY to ₹27.65 lakh crore. SME the standout at +21%, Agri at +19.7%, Retail Personal at +15.2% (Home Loan market share: 28.1%).

Corporate book grew +14.8%; overall domestic mix is 42.5% Retail / 35% Corporate / 13.2% SME / 9.3% Agri — well-balanced. International advances up +20% YoY at ₹7.43 lakh crore.

Management guiding for system credit growth of 13–15% in FY27, with SBI expected to outperform given its reach and digital edge.

📱 Digital & Franchise Moat

YONO platform now has 10.02 crore registered users; 66% of new savings accounts opened digitally. Alternate channels handle 98.7% of all transactions — up from 98.2% in FY25. New YONO crossed 4 crore registrations.

Savings deposits of ₹18.8 lakh crore — nearly 3× the next largest bank. CASA ratio: 39.46% domestic. ~63,000 new savings accounts opened daily. This unmatched liability franchise is SBI's most durable competitive moat.

Declared dividend: ₹17.35/share (record date May 16, 2026), reflecting confidence in sustaining capital generation while rewarding shareholders.
