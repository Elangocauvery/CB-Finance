Titan — The Conglomerate Model

Multi-Brand Architecture

  • Tanishq — flagship mass-premium, 22K/18K mix

Golden Harvest Scheme (GHS)

  • ~7–8 million active subscribers (installment savings)
  • ₹3,600 Cr deferred liability = captive demand float
  • Customers locked 10–11 months before purchase

Manufacturing Moat

  • Hosur factory — India's largest vertically integrated plant
  • 200+ designers | 1,500+ new designs per quarter

Segment Diversification

  • Watches + Wearables: 7.5% revenue, 14.8% EBIT margin

Kalyan — The Franchise Scale Model

FOCO Hyper-Expansion

  • Franchisee funds capex; Kalyan owns inventory + ops
  • ~55% of India showrooms franchise-owned
  • Target: 800+ showrooms by FY27
  • New store payback: 18–24 months

My Kalyan Sub-Network

  • 1,800+ collection centres in Tier 3/4 towns
  • 4M+ installment scheme subscribers

Middle East (USD Revenue)

  • 62 showrooms: UAE, Oman, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain
  • FY26 ME revenue: ~₹4,200 Cr (~14% of total)

Candere Digital Arm

  • Online-first platform, reached profitability FY25
  • ~4% of India revenues; improving

SGIL — The Jewellery Manufacturer Model

Core Business

  • Jewellery manufacturing — bridal focus, 22K/18K gold
  • B2B to retailers + emerging own brand channel
  • Current capacity: 2,700 kg/annum (Chennai)
  • Craftsmanship + design as key differentiator

Revenue Economics

  • Revenue = volume × gold price + making charges
  • Margin expansion via higher bridal/premium mix
  • Gold price tailwind = direct revenue and margin kicker

Expansion Strategy

  • 3 plants post-expansion: Chennai + Jaipur + Mumbai
  • Jaipur: 1,200 kg (North India bridal market access)
  • Mumbai: 4,000 kg (scale + premium market)
  • Post-FY27: ~7,900 kg = 3x current capacity

Customer Acquisition

  • New bridal wedding segment customers
  • Preferred supplier for mid-size regional jewellers
  • Low overhead vs branded retailers = price competitive

Buyer Analysis & Changing Trends

Customer segments, evolving preferences, and demand shifts

Revenue by Customer Segment

Purchase Occasion Mix

Key Buyer Shifts

1. Studded/Diamond Preference Rising
22K plain → 18K studded. Titan studded mix 35% (FY26) vs 27% (FY22). Higher margin play driven by younger buyers and gifting culture.
2. Lightweight Daily-Wear Boom
Working women driving office-wear gold. Mia, CaratLane boom. Daily wear now 22% of Titan revenues in FY26.
3. Online Research → Offline Purchase
73% of buyers research online first. CaratLane try-at-home + in-store pickup = winning model.
4. SGIL Bridal Mix Rising
Bridal jewellery share at SGIL growing — contributing to higher realisation, better gross margins (11.2%), and larger order book.
5. Lab-Grown Diamond Watch
LGD prices down 70% in 4 years. Risk to studded margin over FY27–29.
6. BIS Hallmarking → Organised Shift
Mandatory hallmarking since 2021. Organised share: 12% (FY20) → 18% (FY26). All three companies benefit.
7. Gold as Investment
Gold coins/bars up 45% in FY26. High prices trigger exchange/upgrade behaviour across all players.
8. NRI Diaspora Surge
Indian gold 25–30% cheaper than buying abroad. Kalyan Gulf + Tanishq USA capturing this NRI demand.

Why No GML for SGIL?

  • GML requires strong credit rating and large-scale operations
  • SGIL as a small-cap manufacturer lacks the balance sheet for bank GML lines
  • Gold purchased via cash or short-term working capital loans
  • Post-expansion (7,900 kg/yr), SGIL may qualify for GML — would improve margins ~1–1.5%

Expansion Funding Watch

  • Jaipur + Mumbai plants = significant capex commitment
  • Risk: equity dilution or higher debt to fund expansion
  • Monitor next 2 quarters for funding announcements

Gold Metal Loan (GML) — How It Works

Mechanics

  • Borrow physical gold from banks — not cash
  • Interest: 2.5–4.5% p.a. vs 8–10% for cash loans
  • Manufactured into jewellery, repaid at maturity (30–180 days)
  • No gold price exposure on borrowed quantity

Structural Moat

  • Small jewellers cannot access GML (no creditworthiness)
  • Titan saves ~₹285 Cr/yr vs cash borrowing
  • Permanent cost advantage vs unorganised sector

GML Risks

  • Gold price rally → repayment in gold more expensive
  • RBI can restrict GML limits or increase margins
  • Kalyan's higher GML/sales ratio = slightly more exposed

Working Capital

  • Inventory days: Titan ~130 | Kalyan ~96 (better turnover)
  • Titan hedges ~30–40% of gold risk via MCX futures
  • GHS prepayments = net WC benefit for Titan

If Mumbai and Jaipur plants deliver on schedule, FY27/28 earnings could surprise significantly. This is a high-risk, high-reward small-cap play. Gold price is the key binary risk.
✅ STRENGTHS
  • Q4 PAT +465% — explosive growth
  • EBITDA margin 10.2% — approaching best-in-class
  • 3x capacity by FY27 = 3x revenue potential
  • Bridal focus = premium mix, higher realisation
  • Gold price tailwind flowing fully to margins
  • New customer acquisitions accelerating
⚠ RISKS
  • Small cap — limited liquidity
  • Plant execution risk (Jaipur + Mumbai)
  • Gold correction = revenue + margin hit
  • No distribution moat (pure manufacturer)
  • B2B bridal customer concentration
  • QoQ production dip (535→463 kg) to watch
CB View: For risk-tolerant investors with 2–3 year horizon. Initiate small position; add on plant commissioning confirmation. Monitor Q1 FY27 production numbers closely. Stop-loss trigger: capacity delay beyond Q4 FY27.
