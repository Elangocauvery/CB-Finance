Financial Results Deep-Dive — Bajaj Auto · Standalone vs Consolidated
Read the consolidated growth rate with caution: The +65.1% consolidated revenue / +45.9% consolidated PAT headlines are flagged by Bajaj itself as not comparable YoY. Following completion of full control over Bajaj Auto International Holdings AG (formerly Pierer Bajaj AG, KTM's parent) on 18 Nov 2025, the group began consolidating those results with a one-quarter lag — so Q1 FY27 consolidated figures include KTM-group revenue/costs absent from the Q1 FY26 base. The standalone numbers (+37% revenue, +42% PAT) are the clean like-for-like read.

Business Analysis — TVS Motor · Research analysis only, not a recommendation
1. Broad-based growth, not a single-lever story
Every major segment grew double digits YoY: 2W +27%, 3W +48%, EV +85%, exports +47%. Structurally healthier than a narrow, single-segment beat.
Structural Positive
2. Profit growth outran revenue growth — partly non-operating
PAT +64.5% vs revenue +33.5% — a ₹149.6 Cr fair-valuation gain sits in other income. Separate the core-operations story from the one-off mark-to-market boost.
Read With Care
3. EV leadership real but the gap to #2 is shrinking
Bajaj's Chetak grew share faster (80.9% YoY vs TVS's 76.6%) in June 2026, narrowing the gap to ~3,800 units/month. Ather is also compounding quickly.
Watch Closely
4. Fresh ₹1,000 Cr debt raise alongside record results
Signals active capital deployment (EV/3W capacity, working capital, international expansion) — a modest, planned leverage step versus TVS's historically conservative balance sheet.
Neutral / Monitor
5. International business is a genuine second engine
Record exports (+47%) plus Indonesia entry and a Bologna design/engineering CoE show exports compounding roughly in line with the domestic business.
Structural Positive
6. Margin trajectory is the key unresolved question
Detailed EBITDA/cost breakup not yet disclosed as of this writing. Street pre-results estimate was ~12.1% margin (down from 12.5%) on cost/mix pressure — the single most important number to track post-concall.

Business Analysis — Bajaj Auto · Research analysis only, not a recommendation
1. Standalone numbers are the real story — and they're strong
Revenue +37%, EBITDA +45%, margin expansion of 120bps to 20.9%, PAT +42% — broad-based across ICE, EV, exports and CVs, with real operating leverage alongside 29% volume growth.
Structural Positive
2. Consolidated growth rates are not what they appear

Read With Care
3. Exports crossing 7 lakh units is a genuine milestone
First-ever 700,000-unit export quarter, with 2W exports +52% and CV exports +69%, signals real recovery in traditional export strongholds.
Structural Positive

Management said EV demand continues to exceed production capacity — validates demand, but near-term EV revenue growth hinges on how fast Bajaj scales manufacturing.
Watch Closely
5. Sequential softness explains the muted stock reaction
Both standalone and consolidated PAT declined QoQ from a strong Q4 FY26, despite YoY beats — markets often price sequential momentum over YoY optics.
Neutral / Monitor
6. KTM integration is the multi-year swing factor
Domestic KTM+Triumph revenue +60% is an early positive, but the European restructuring is still a multi-year story that will materially shape consolidated profitability.

SWOT & Competitive Moat — TVS Motor · Post Q1 FY27
Strengths
  • #1 in India's electric 2W market, 10 L+ cumulative EV units
  • Record, broad-based Q1 FY27 growth across all segments
  • Diversified global footprint — 90 countries
  • Apache crossed 7 million customers — deep brand loyalty
Weaknesses
  • EBITDA margin structurally lower than Eicher/Bajaj
  • Part of PAT growth from non-operating fair-value gain
  • Rich valuation (TTM P/E ~53×)
  • Taking on incremental debt vs historically conservative balance sheet
Opportunities
  • Continued EV 2W category growth (+75% YoY, June 2026)
  • New international markets (Indonesia) and premium brands (Norton)
  • Commercial/B2B EV logistics via King Kargo
  • Global engineering scale-up via Bologna CoE
Threats
  • Bajaj's Chetak closing the EV market-share gap faster
  • Ather compounding share quickly among top-3 EV players
  • Raw-material inflation and richer EV mix pressuring margins
  • Export markets carry FX/geopolitical exposure
SWOT & Competitive Moat — Bajaj Auto · Post Q1 FY27
Strengths
  • Record standalone quarter — revenue, EBITDA, margin all expanding
  • World's largest 3W manufacturer; #2 domestic 2W by volume
  • Export franchise crossing 7 lakh units/quarter for the first time
  • Chetak EV profitable and demand-constrained, not lacking demand
Weaknesses
  • Consolidated financials temporarily hard to compare YoY
  • Sequential PAT decline vs a strong Q4 FY26
  • KTM-group European turnaround still unproven at full profitability
  • Rich valuation (P/E ~25.5×, premium to peer median)
Opportunities
  • Chetak capacity expansion could unlock unmet EV demand
  • Continued export market-share gains as EM currencies stabilise
  • KTM/Triumph India-manufacturing shift could improve margins
  • FY27 guidance clarity from AGM/concall commentary
Threats
  • KTM/European restructuring execution risk
  • Competitive EV pressure from TVS iQube and Ather
  • Export markets carry FX, political and tariff exposure
  • Rare-earth magnet/component supply constraints could recur

Key Analytical Takeaway

Both companies delivered genuinely strong, broad-based Q1 FY27 quarters — but the profiles differ meaningfully. TVS is the higher-volume, higher-growth-rate, richly-valued EV leader whose margin story is still an open question this quarter. Bajaj is the larger, structurally higher-margin (20.9% EBITDA margin), lower-multiple business, whose headline consolidated growth numbers need to be read net of the KTM consolidation effect. On a like-for-like operating basis, Bajaj's standalone EBITDA margin profile is currently the stronger one of the two — while TVS's EV market leadership and international diversification remain its distinguishing structural strengths.
