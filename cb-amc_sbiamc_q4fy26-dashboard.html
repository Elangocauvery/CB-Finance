Coverage Snapshot — Q4 FY26 (Jan–Mar 2026): This CB Research dashboard covers India's five listed AMCs plus the largest unlisted AMC now going public. ICICI Prudential AMC and Nippon Life India AMC (NAM India) delivered the cleanest Q4s — both grew revenue ~20-30% YoY with PAT up double digits, NAM India the fastest-growing top-10 AMC in India. 💵 Revenue Streams & Other Income — How Each AMC Actually Earns
An AMC's Operating Revenue is management fees — a basis-point rate charged on AUM, deducted daily from scheme NAV. Other Income is a completely separate thing: the mark-to-market gain/loss plus interest/dividend earned on the AMC's own proprietary balance-sheet investment book (built from retained profits, plus SEBI-mandated "skin in the game" seed capital in its own new schemes). Other income has nothing to do with how well client money is managed — it's a treasury function sitting on top of the core business — which is exactly why it caused most of the Q4 FY26 PAT volatility seen elsewhere in this dashboard. AMC-யின் இயக்க வருவாய் என்பது AUM மீதான fee. மற்ற வருமானம் என்பது AMC-யின் சொந்த முதலீட்டு போர்ட்ஃபோலியோவின் MTM லாபம்/நஷ்டம் — வாடிக்கையாளர் பணத்துடன் தொடர்பில்லை.

📈 The Structural Chain: Demat Accounts → SIP → AUM → AMC Fee Income
1️⃣ Demat account growth
India's demat account base has grown from under 4 crore in 2019 to roughly 19+ crore by FY26 — a function of UPI-linked KYC, discount brokers, and smartphone penetration. Every new demat/MF-folio holder is a potential lifetime AUM contributor for an AMC, at near-zero marginal acquisition cost once the digital rails exist (this is exactly what HDFC AMC's "97% digital transactions" and NAM India's "77% digital purchases" figures represent).
2️⃣ SIP culture entrenchment
Monthly SIP inflows crossed ~₹25,000+ Cr industry-wide in FY26 (NAM India alone logged a record ₹32,100 Cr monthly SIP book in March 2026; UTI AMC crossed 14.5 lakh gross new SIP registrations for the year). SIPs are "sticky" recurring revenue — they smooth AMC fee income across market cycles and reduce redemption volatility versus lump-sum inflows, which is why every AMC in this dashboard reports SIP metrics as a headline KPI.
3️⃣ B-30 & retail deepening
Beyond-Top-30-city (B-30) AUM is growing faster than metro AUM for most AMCs — NAM India's B-30 assets rose 28% YoY to ₹1.43 lakh Cr (20.1% share vs 18.2% industry average); SBI Funds Management's B-30 AUM stood at ₹2.77 lakh Cr (22.8% of its MF AUM). This is the demat-culture story reaching Tier-2/3 India — new investors there behave differently (higher redemption sensitivity in downturns), which is why CB flags B-30 concentration as a risk factor, not just an opportunity.
4️⃣ Operating leverage payoff
Because AMC cost structures (staff, technology, compliance) are largely fixed in the short-to-medium term, revenue growth from rising AUM drops through to profit at a much higher rate — visible in ICICI Prudential AMC's 76.5% Q4 operating margin (highest in its history) and SBI Funds Management's industry-lowest 0.08% opex-to-QAAUM ratio. This operating leverage is the core reason AMC stocks often re-rate ahead of a rising market: the same demat-account growth that lifts AUM also lifts margins.

C. Because regulators require AMCs to report operating profit as the primary metric.
CB's view: Answer B. This dashboard's Q4 FY26 data set is a clean illustration: four of five listed AMCs (HDFC, ICICI Pru, ABSL, UTI) saw PAT growth diverge meaningfully from operating-revenue growth in the same quarter, purely because each holds a proprietary investment book (largely debt/liquid instruments plus some equity) marked to market every quarter-end. PAT is still the number that ultimately matters for EPS and dividends — CB isn't dismissing it — but for judging whether the underlying AUM-gathering and fee business is healthy, operating revenue and operating profit are the cleaner signal. 🏆 CB Research Q4 FY26 AMC Sector Verdict

ICICI Prudential AMC and Nippon Life India AMC stand out as the cleanest Q4 FY26 stories — both combined genuine operating-revenue and operating-profit growth (not just AUM mark-up) with PAT growth that held up despite sector-wide "other income" headwinds. HDFC AMC remains the scale leader and its core business (fee income, margin, digital adoption) is arguably as strong as ever — its headline PAT miss is a market-conditions artefact, not a franchise problem. Aditya Birla Sun Life AMC is steady but the smallest of the listed majors, with margin softening worth monitoring into FY27. UTI AMC is the one name in this set requiring real patience — its Q4 net loss and cost-transformation story mean the next 2-3 quarters of normalised earnings will matter more than this one. SBI Funds Management, going public this week, brings the largest AUM base and the best cost-efficiency in the sector to the market, but as a pure OFS its listing is an ownership/liquidity event for SBI-Amundi, not a capital-raise for growth — investors should judge it on its RHP-disclosed 21% PAT growth and 15.3% market-leading share, tempered by the absence of any public quarterly disclosure history.

📌 What every AMC investor should track from here
Operating revenue > headline PAT
Separate fee-income (AUM-linked, recurring) growth from proprietary-book MTM swings every quarter before reacting to a "profit miss" or "profit beat."
QAAUM market-share trend, not just AUM growth
Every AMC's AUM is growing in FY26's rising-industry tide (AMFI industry AUM ₹73.7 lakh Cr, +12%); the real differentiator is who is gaining share — NAM India (+63 bps) and ICICI Pru AMC (+bps gains) are; UTI AMC has structurally lagged.
Digital transaction share as a demat-culture proxy
Rising digital-transaction percentages (HDFC AMC 97%, NAM India 77%, UTI 89.5% online) signal which AMCs are best-placed to capture incremental demat/SIP growth at low marginal cost.
Regulatory/TER overhang
New TER rules effective April 2026 are estimated to shave ~3-4 bps off equity-AUM yields sector-wide (per NAM India and ABSL AMC commentary) — watch FY27 Q1 margins for the actual pass-through impact.
