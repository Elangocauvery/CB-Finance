🚀 Why Hotels Are Performing — 9 Key Reasons
✈️
1. India Domestic Travel at All-Time High — Air passenger traffic crossed 15 Cr in FY26. Tier 2-3 city hotels seeing demand surge as middle-class travel democratises.
💍
2. Wedding Season Boom (Q4 = Peak) — Record auspicious dates in Jan-Mar 2026, average wedding spend ₹50-75 Lakh. Premium hotels fully booked months ahead for banquets and MICE events.
🏢
3. Business Travel Recovery + MICE — Corporate India expanding; conferences, seminars, product launches back at full scale. Business hotels running 75-85% occupancy.
📉
4. Supply Shortage = Pricing Power — New hotel supply takes 4-5 years. Demand outpacing supply → RevPAR/ARR keeps rising without occupancy trade-off.
🌍
5. International Tourist Arrivals Recovering — Foreign arrivals +18% YoY in FY26. Golden Triangle, Goa, Kerala driving luxury hotel demand.
⚙️
6. Operating Leverage Magic — Fixed cost base stays constant. Every extra ₹100 RevPAR adds ₹60-70 to EBITDA. Margins expanding rapidly on incremental revenue.
🛎️
7. Premiumisation Trend — Indians upgrading from budget to premium stays. Luxury hotel segment growing 18-20% vs economy 8-10%. Taj, Marriott, Hyatt brands gaining disproportionately.
💻
8. Asset-Light Model Scalability — IHCL (68% asset-light), Lemon Tree (fee-based) growing room inventory without heavy capex. RoCE improving dramatically.
🇮🇳
9. India's Hospitality Moment — G20 legacy, "Incredible India 2.0", new airports in Tier 2 cities (Ayodhya, Shirdi), religious tourism (Prayagraj Kumbh Mela). India becoming top-5 global travel destination.

⚠️ Sector-Wide Risks
📉 Valuation Stretch: Sector EV/EBITDA 18-28x — comparable to 2011-14 downturn highs. Golden cycle may already be priced in for premium names.
🌍 Global Recession: Any US/Europe slowdown reduces MICE corporate bookings and inbound foreign tourism significantly.
⚖️ GST/Regulatory: Government GST policy on hotel stays (28% for 5-star). Any change impacts ARR realisation.
🏗️ Supply Pipeline: Hotel supply in India at 5-year high additions from 2026 — could moderate RevPAR growth from FY28.
💰 Input Costs: Food and beverage inflation, hospitality staff wage competition, energy costs rising.
🦠 Disruption Risk: COVID-type tail risk — hotels most directly impacted by any travel restriction.
