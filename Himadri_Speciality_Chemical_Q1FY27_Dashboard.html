📋 Q1 FY27 Result Summary

Himadri Speciality Chemical reported its highest-ever quarterly revenue, EBITDA and PAT for the quarter ended 30 June 2026. Consolidated revenue from operations rose 28.04% YoY to ₹1,431.88 Cr (₹1,118.29 Cr in Q1 FY26), and grew 11.19% sequentially over Q4 FY26 (₹1,287.76 Cr). Total income, including ₹56.31 Cr of other income (up from ₹26.68 Cr YoY), reached ₹1,488.19 Cr, up 29.98% YoY. EBITDA rose 33% YoY to ₹313.11 Cr with margin expanding to 21.9–22% from ~21%, and PAT grew 27% YoY to ₹228.43 Cr (16% PAT margin) — driven by a favourable product mix, ramp-up of speciality materials, and the company's diversification into higher-margin, technology-intensive new energy materials. The Board (meeting held 15 July 2026) simultaneously approved a fresh ₹368 Cr capex tranche spanning Super Speciality Carbon Black, India's first Carbon Nano Tube (CNT) facility, and doubling of Anthraquinone/Carbazole capacity — funded via internal accruals.

🏆 Highest-Ever Quarterly Print

Revenue, EBITDA and PAT all hit record quarterly highs in Q1 FY27, continuing an unbroken multi-quarter uptrend in both volumes and margins since Q1 FY25.

🔋 New Energy Pivot Gathering Pace

200 TPA anode facility commissioned Apr 2026; 2,000 TPA LFP cathode line targeted Q3 FY27; 200 TPA CNT plant (₹70 Cr) targeted Q4 FY27 — India's first indigenous CNT line.

⚠️ Watch: Valuation Has Re-Rated Sharply

Stock is up ~63% over 1 year and trades at ~46x trailing earnings / ~7.3x book — well above its 10-year average multiple, pricing in significant execution success on new-energy verticals that are still pre-scale.

🏗️ Q1 FY27 Capex Announcement (Board Meeting, 15 Jul 2026)

The Board approved a fresh ₹368 Cr capex tranche funded through internal accruals: ₹170 Cr for the Super Speciality Carbon Black conversion, ₹70 Cr for India's first Carbon Nano Tube facility, and ₹128 Cr to double Anthraquinone and Carbazole production capacity. This sits alongside the larger, multi-year new-energy platform build-out (anode, LFP cathode, CNT) and the Birla Tyres turnaround capex, which some market reports aggregate into total announced/multi-year capex figures ranging from roughly ₹1,000–1,600 Cr — investors should treat the larger figures as cumulative multi-project, multi-year commitments rather than a single Q1 outlay.

🔍 Valuation Context — What the Multiple Is Pricing In

At ~46x trailing earnings and ~7.3x book value, Himadri trades well above its own 10-year historical average multiple and above most large-cap speciality/commodity chemical peers (Aarti, SRF, Deepak Nitrite typically trade in the 15–35x range in FY26). The premium reflects the market pricing in: (a) continuation of the ~30% EBITDA/PAT growth trajectory seen over the last five quarters, and (b) meaningful future contribution from the new-energy materials pipeline (LFP cathode, CNT, silicon-carbon anode via Sicona, IBC stake) — verticals that are still pre-commercial-scale as of Q1 FY27. A re-rating case built substantially on unproven new verticals carries higher execution risk than one built on the mature coal-tar-pitch/carbon-black core, and any commissioning delay (LFP Q3 FY27, CNT Q4 FY27, SSCB Q4 FY28) or slower-than-expected ramp could compress the multiple even if the core business continues performing.

🔍 Relative Positioning

Within this peer set, Himadri stands out on profit growth momentum (FY26 PAT +36%, Q1 FY27 PAT +27% YoY) and margin resilience (EBITDA margin ~21–22%, among the higher end of the group) even as pure-play carbon black peer PCBL saw sharp FY26 margin compression. Himadri is smaller than Aarti Industries by revenue but is compounding PAT faster and carries much lower leverage. Its key differentiator versus every listed peer here is the new-energy-materials diversification (anode, LFP cathode, CNT) — a call option on the EV/battery supply chain that none of the pure coal-tar/carbon-black peers currently have at comparable scale.

🔲 SWOT Analysis Himadri Speciality Chemical Ltd · Q1 FY27
✅ Strengths
  • #1 in India in Coal Tar Pitch and Naphthalene; largest player in SNF/PCE — deep backward integration from coal tar distillation to finished specialty products
  • Five consecutive quarters of record/near-record revenue, EBITDA and PAT (Q1 FY27 highest-ever on all three)
  • Strong return ratios — ROCE 22.1%, ROE 17.8% (FY26), well above cost of capital
  • 8 zero-liquid-discharge plants; >90% in-house green power — favourable ESG/regulatory positioning
  • Export reach to 49 countries via two overseas subsidiaries (Hong Kong, China)
  • Low leverage relative to net worth despite recent capex step-up (₹770 Cr debt vs ₹4,706 Cr net worth)
⚠️ Weaknesses
  • Core Coal Tar Pitch/Carbon Black business remains commodity-linked, sensitive to input-cost (coal tar, crude derivatives) and cyclicality
  • FY26 top-line growth of just ~1% shows core-business volume growth has plateaued; profit growth has outpaced revenue growth
  • Cash conversion cycle has lengthened from 66 days (FY23) to 103 days (FY26) — rising working-capital intensity
  • FY26 Free Cash Flow turned negative (-₹63 Cr) on heavy capex/investing outflow (-₹963 Cr)
  • Birla Tyres remains a turnaround story, not yet a proven profit contributor
  • New-energy materials (LFP, CNT, SSCB) are all pre-commercial-scale — near-zero current revenue contribution despite high market expectations
🚀 Opportunities
  • India's first indigenous CNT facility (targeted Q4 FY27) — first-mover position in a high-growth, import-substitution material
  • LFP cathode active material ramp (2,000 TPA Q3 FY27 → long-term ambition of 200,000 TPA) rides the global EV/battery localisation wave and PLI-linked demand
  • Anthraquinone/Carbazole capacity doubling reduces India's import dependence on dye/pigment intermediates from China
  • Strategic stakes in Sicona (silicon-carbon anode, Australia) and International Battery Company (USA) provide technology optionality without full capital commitment
  • China+1 supply-chain diversification benefits Indian speciality carbon-chemical exporters generally
  • Birla Tyres entry into passenger car radial (EV/SUV) segment (targeted FY28) could open a large adjacent market if the turnaround executes
🔺 Threats
  • Valuation (~46x trailing P/E, ~7.3x book) has run well ahead of proven new-energy earnings — any commissioning delay (LFP/CNT/SSCB) risks a sharp de-rating
  • Global LFP/CNT/anode markets are increasingly crowded, with Chinese and other Asian producers holding significant cost and scale advantages
  • Coal tar and crude-linked feedstock price volatility can compress core-segment margins with limited pass-through in weak-demand periods
  • Rising leverage trajectory (debt +146% in FY26) if sustained across multiple large capex programmes simultaneously could pressure the balance sheet
  • Execution risk is concentrated on a small number of new facilities (anode, LFP, CNT, SSCB) commissioning within a tight FY27–FY28 window
  • Tyre segment (Birla Tyres) faces intense competition from established domestic and international players
🏰 Competitive Moat Sources of Durable Advantage — and Where They're Still Unproven
SCALE & BACKWARD INTEGRATION
India's Deepest Coal-Tar Value Chain
Himadri is the No.1 coal tar pitch producer and the largest Naphthalene and SNF/PCE player in India, with integration running from raw coal-tar distillation through to finished, higher-margin specialty products (naphthalene balls, debonders, speciality oils). This scale and integration is difficult and capital-intensive for a new entrant to replicate, particularly given the environmental permitting burden of coal-tar distillation in India.
Durable — Core Business
TECHNOLOGY / R&D
Decade-Plus In-House R&D in Advanced Carbon Materials
The anode-materials facility commissioned in April 2026 draws on "more than a decade" of in-house R&D per management commentary, and the indigenous CNT technology was developed in-house rather than licensed — positioning Himadri among a select group of global CNT manufacturers if the 200 TPA facility commissions as planned in Q4 FY27. This head start is real, but the moat is only as durable as the company's ability to scale ahead of well-funded Chinese and global battery-materials competitors.
Emerging — Unproven at Scale
ESG / REGULATORY POSITIONING
Zero-Liquid-Discharge Plants & Green Power
Eight zero-liquid-discharge facilities and >90% in-house/green power usage give Himadri a structural cost and compliance advantage as Indian and export-market environmental norms tighten, particularly for chemical exporters selling into the EU and other regulated markets. This is a genuine, multi-year-built advantage that is hard for late movers to catch up on quickly.
Durable — Structural
⚠️ Where the Moat Is Still Being Tested

The core coal-tar-pitch/carbon-black moat is real but is a commodity-adjacent business with only moderate pricing power — margin expansion in Q1 FY27 came significantly from product mix and specialty-material ramp-up rather than from pure pricing power in the base business. The new-energy-materials moat (CNT, LFP, silicon-carbon anode) is a genuine first-mover position in India but remains globally contested: China dominates LFP cathode and CNT production at far greater scale, and Himadri's 200–2,000 TPA facilities are a fraction of the capacity that global leaders already operate. Investors should distinguish between the proven, durable moat in the legacy carbon-chemicals business and the promising-but-unproven optionality in new energy materials — the current valuation multiple appears to already be pricing in success in the latter.
