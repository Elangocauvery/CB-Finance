Group Structure at a Glance

Adani Green Energy (AGEL)

Pure-play renewable generator — solar, wind, hybrid + battery storage (BESS). 20.1 GW operational capacity. No distribution/retail exposure — pure generation-and-sell-under-PPA model.

Adani Energy Solutions (AESL)

India's largest private transmission + distribution (Mumbai via AEML) + smart metering + emerging Energy Solutions Platform (renewable tie-ups, C&I, data centres).

Adani Power (APL)

India's largest private thermal generator, expanding toward 45 GW; entering nuclear energy via new Adani Atomic Energy subsidiaries; inorganic growth through Jaiprakash Associates (JAL) insolvency assets.

CB Research Quick Take

All three entities delivered strong Q1 FY27 prints on the back of India's hot summer power demand (peak demand hit a record 270.8 GW in May 2026) and continued capex-led capacity build-out. AESL's profit growth was the standout (130% YoY), helped by the Energy Solutions Platform segment turning from near-nil to ₹590 Cr in segment profit. APL's headline profit included a one-time ₹1,386 Cr prior-period regulatory recognition — core continuing profitability, while still strong, grew more moderately. AGEL's capacity crossed the 20 GW milestone with industry-leading margins, though the stock corrected post-results on profit booking. Business Model & Segments

Solar

Utility-scale solar parks, anchored by the Khavda RE Park in Gujarat — being developed as the world's largest renewable energy park (~30 GW planned).

Wind & Hybrid

Wind and solar-wind hybrid assets that improve capacity utilisation and firm up round-the-clock (RTC) power supply to offtakers.

Revenue Model

Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) — Build-out Roadmap

Current Scale

3,551 MWh (3.55 GWh) commissioned at Khavda as of Q1 FY27 — the world's largest single-location BESS deployment outside China, built in roughly 10 months of on-site construction.

Near-term Target

An additional ~15 GWh of BESS capacity planned by March 2027 (FY27-end), taking AGEL well past the 10 GWh milestone flagged for FY27.

2030 Target

50 GWh of total BESS capacity over the next five years — directly supporting AGEL's revised 50 GW renewable energy target by 2030 by converting intermittent solar/wind into firm, round-the-clock (RTC) power.

Group Structure — Not a Formal JV

BESS is being developed under an Adani Group-wide "integrated utility platform" spanning AGEL (current implementer), AESL and Adani Power. AESL has stated the Group will evaluate future roles for AESL/APL in BESS as it scales — but as of Q1 FY27 there is no formal external battery-technology JV (market speculation around potential tie-ups with global cell makers like CATL or BYD remains unconfirmed).

Key USPs

Scale & Speed

Crossed 20 GW operational capacity; added 4,327 MW greenfield capacity in the trailing year — fastest capacity ramp among Indian RE players.

Margin Leadership

94% EBITDA margin in the power supply segment — among the highest in the global renewable energy sector, reflecting asset quality and low opex intensity of solar/wind.

ESG Track Record

Retained the highest CRISIL ESG score in the Indian power sector for the 5th consecutive year; score improved from 66 to 69 in FY26.

Vertical Integration

Benefits from Adani Group's in-house EPC, module/cell manufacturing and land-bank advantages at Khavda, supporting cost leadership.

CB Research View — AGEL

  • Strength: Capacity growth (27% YoY) and margin expansion are both intact — a rare combination as most RE developers see margin dilution while scaling.
  • Watch: Stock fell 2.7–4% on results day despite the beat — likely profit-booking after a strong run and/or market digesting the pace of capex vs free cash flow generation.
  • Structural driver: BESS buildout (targeting 50,000 MWh by 2030) is the next leg of the story — converts AGEL from a pure intermittent-power seller into a firm/RTC power provider, opening higher-value contracts.
  • Risk: Revenue concentration in long-tenor discom PPAs means counterparty (state discom) financial health remains a key monitorable — see the Mahavitaran IPO tab.

Forward Capacity Roadmap

Target: ~45 GW by FY31–32

Up from ~18.3 GW currently — a ~2.5x expansion — backed by a capex programme exceeding ₹2 lakh crore (₹2 trillion), disclosed at the FY26 AGM (June 2026).

Brownfield Expansion

Six Ultra Supercritical Thermal Power Plants (USCTPPs) of 1,600 MW each under construction/planned at existing sites — Singrauli-Mahan (MP), Raipur, Raigarh and Korba (Chhattisgarh), and Kawai (Rajasthan).

Greenfield: Mirzapur, UP

A new 1,600 MW greenfield USCTPP planned at Mirzapur, Uttar Pradesh — extending APL's footprint into a new state.

Diversification: Nuclear & Hydro

Targeting 10 GW of nuclear capacity by 2035 via Adani Atomic Energy Ltd, plus a 5,000 MW hydropower JV with Bhutan's Druk Green Power Corporation — moving beyond pure thermal generation.

Key USPs

Scale & Cost Leadership

India's largest private thermal IPP — scale gives it fuel-sourcing and O&M cost advantages plus bidding competitiveness (e.g. won a 1,600 MW MSEDCL PSA at ₹5.30/kWh, one of the lowest recent thermal tariffs).

Demand Tailwind

Record peak power demand of 270.8 GW in May 2026 amid a hot summer; energy consumption rose 8.4% YoY to 485.4 BU in the quarter, filling APL's expanded capacity.

Balance Sheet Discipline

DSCR improved to 6.03x (from 5.47x); ISCR at 8.61x — strong debt servicing headroom even amid an aggressive capex and M&A programme.

CB Research View — Adani Power

  • Strength: Highest-ever quarterly EBITDA on a continuing basis, with power demand tailwinds and higher tariff realization supporting the core business independent of one-off items.
  • Watch: Headline PAT growth (47.2%) is flattered by a one-time ₹1,386 Cr prior-period recognition; strip this out and growth is still healthy but more moderate — useful to normalize when comparing YoY.
  • Capital raise: ₹15,000 Cr QIP + higher borrowing limit signals aggressive capex intent toward the 45 GW target and possibly further JAL-style inorganic bolt-ons — modestly dilutive near-term but capacity-accretive.
  • Optionality: Nuclear energy subsidiaries are a long-dated, low-probability-near-term but high-optionality diversification — not yet revenue-relevant but signals strategic intent as India opens nuclear to private players.

CB Research Comparative Take

On pure YoY profit momentum, AESL led the pack (130%), followed by Adani Power (47.2%, partly one-off aided) and AGEL (19.3%, off a higher margin base). On absolute scale, Adani Power remains the largest profit contributor of the trio by a wide margin. AGEL stands out for margin quality (94% segment EBITDA margin), while AESL stands out for breadth — every single segment grew profit this quarter. All three share a common structural link worth watching together: their reliance on state discom counterparties for long-term revenue visibility, which is precisely why the Mahavitaran IPO (next tab) matters to the group as a whole.

CB Research's own fundamentals-based scorecard for Q1 FY27 — earnings quality (growth + margin) as the primary benchmark, with debt and cash flow assessed separately.

Methodology

Revenue Growth — 20%

YoY revenue growth vs company's own trend and sector context.

EBITDA Growth & Margin — 25%

Combines YoY EBITDA growth with absolute margin quality — rewards companies growing profitably, not just growing.

Net Profit Growth & Margin — 25%

Combines YoY PAT growth with net margin, adjusted for one-off/non-recurring items where disclosed.

Debt / Leverage — 15%

Assessed via debt-service coverage (DSCR/ISCR), net debt trend, and net worth cushion.

Cash Flow — 15%

Cash profit generation and its growth — a check on whether reported profit is converting to real cash.

Maharashtra Mahavitaran (MSEDCL) IPO — Impact Assessment

Maharashtra's largest state discom is being split and readied for a public listing — here's what it means for AGEL, AESL and APL.

What's Happening

The Split

Maharashtra's Cabinet (CM Devendra Fadnavis) approved splitting MSEDCL into two entities: Mahavitaran (industrial, commercial & domestic consumers — the IPO-bound entity) and MSEB Solar Agro Power Ltd (MSAPL) (agricultural consumers, to be served via distributed solar).

Balance Sheet Clean-up

The demerger strips ~₹76,000 Cr of agri-related dues off Mahavitaran's books. The state is also issuing ₹32,679 Cr of 15-year state-guaranteed bonds to refinance MSEDCL's ~₹90,000 Cr debt ahead of listing.

IPO Structure

Fresh equity issuance + Offer for Sale (OFS) by the government, diluting up to ~10% of its stake. Targeted raise: $500 Mn–$1 Bn. Advisors: SBI Capital and Deloitte. Timeline: originally guided for December 2026, with listing expected 6–9 months post-restructuring subject to SEBI clearance.

Scale

Mahavitaran serves ~3.4 crore (34 million) consumers — it is the 2nd largest electricity distribution utility in the world after China's State Grid. FY25 revenue was ₹1,26,069 Cr, up from ₹1,16,377 Cr in FY24.

Company-wise Impact

Adani Green Energy Net Positive

AGEL/Adani Power hold a Letter of Intent to supply 6,600 MW of hybrid solar+thermal power to the Maharashtra state DISCOM. A financially cleaner, better-governed Mahavitaran post-IPO improves counterparty payment discipline on this and future long-tenor PPAs — directly reducing receivables/realization risk, historically one of the biggest overhangs for Indian renewable IPPs selling to state discoms.

Watch: No direct equity or operational linkage — impact is purely via improved counterparty credit quality on existing/future power supply contracts.

Adani Energy Solutions Net Positive

AEML-Distribution (Mumbai suburbs) operates in an adjacent but distinct license area from Mahavitaran (rest of Maharashtra), so this is not a direct competitive threat. A successful, well-subscribed Mahavitaran IPO would set a valuation benchmark for regulated Indian discom assets, which could support a re-rating of AEML-D within AESL's sum-of-the-parts valuation. AESL's Smart Metering and Energy Solutions Platform businesses could also pursue commercial opportunities (smart meter contracts, energy solutions) with a modernising Mahavitaran as it upgrades infrastructure with IPO proceeds.

Watch: A precedent-setting discom listing could also encourage other states to consider distribution reforms/privatisation — a broader tailwind for AESL's core distribution and smart-metering businesses nationally.

Adani Power Net Positive

APL already won a 1,600 MW long-term PSA with MSEDCL (25-year agreement, ₹5.30/kWh, supply from FY30-31) — one of five long-term PSA wins in FY25-26. A financially restructured, IPO-ready Mahavitaran with cleaner receivables and improved payment discipline directly de-risks the revenue stream from this contract and improves confidence in future large-volume PSA bids with the same counterparty.

Watch: Because Mahavitaran's IPO proceeds are earmarked for smart metering and T&D upgrades (not new generation), it does not create incremental competing generation capacity — it mainly strengthens the demand side of APL's existing/contracted supply.

Sector-wide Read-through Structural

Indian state discoms collectively carry over ₹7 lakh Cr in debt/losses, a chronic overhang on generator and transmission cash flows nationally. Mahavitaran — India's largest discom — successfully cleaning its balance sheet and listing would be a landmark proof point that could catalyse similar reforms/listings in other states, gradually improving payment security across the sector that all three Adani entities sell into.
