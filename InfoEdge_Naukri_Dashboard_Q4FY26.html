Business Overview

Info Edge (India) Ltd is India's original internet classifieds company, founded by Sanjeev Bikhchandani in 1995, best known for Naukri.com, India's largest online recruitment platform. But Info Edge today is best understood as two businesses under one roof: (1) a portfolio of four operating consumer-internet verticals — Recruitment (Naukri), Real Estate (99acres), Matchmaking (Jeevansathi + Aisle), and Education (Shiksha, now including wholly-owned Coding Ninjas) — and (2) one of India's most successful early-stage venture investment operations, holding stakes in 135 startups including two of India's biggest consumer-tech IPOs: Eternal (Zomato + Blinkit) and PB Fintech (Policybazaar + Paisabazaar).

Founded 1995 HQ Noida 4 Operating Verticals 135-Company VC Portfolio
The Four Operating Verticals
  • Recruitment Solutions (Naukri): India's dominant online job marketplace — ~58% operating margin, the profit engine funding everything else
  • 99acres: Real estate classifieds — traffic market leader, only recently turned operating-profit positive after years of investment
  • Matchmaking (Jeevansathi + Aisle): Matrimony and dating — smaller, faster-growing, narrowing losses toward breakeven
  • Education (Shiksha + Coding Ninjas): Education discovery and upskilling — facing real AI-search headwinds, offset by the newly-consolidated Coding Ninjas
The Investment Arm — A Different Kind of Business

Since 2007, Info Edge and its managed Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs) — run in partnership with Temasek's MacRitchie Investments — have deployed roughly ₹4,900 Cr across 135 startups, now carried at a fair value of roughly ₹41,300 Cr (an 8.4x multiple, ~33% gross IRR). Its earliest and largest bets — Policybazaar (2008) and Zomato (2010) — are now listed companies in which Info Edge still holds meaningful stakes. This investment book is large enough that Info Edge's own share price frequently moves more on Eternal/PB Fintech stock swings than on Naukri's operating results.

Why This Matters for Investors — The "Two Businesses in One Stock" Problem

Info Edge's consolidated profit and loss statement blends a mature, highly profitable classifieds business with mark-to-market swings on a large listed-equity book. In Q4 FY26 alone, unrealised fair value movements on Eternal (-₹5,874 Cr) and PB Fintech (-₹2,287 Cr) swamped the underlying operating profit entirely, producing a large consolidated comprehensive loss even as the core Naukri/99acres/Jeevansathi/Shiksha businesses were having their best quarter in years. Investors evaluating Info Edge need to separate these two stories — the steady, cash-generative classifieds engine, and the volatile, venture-style investment book — rather than reading the headline P&L at face value.

Shiksha is the one Info Edge business facing a genuine structural headwind: management explicitly attributes declining billings to AI-driven changes in search behaviour — as students increasingly use AI chat tools directly for course/college research rather than clicking through to discovery sites like Shiksha, organic referral traffic has dropped sharply. Info Edge's response is to fold in Coding Ninjas (now a 100% owned subsidiary after the ₹39.9 Cr buyout of the remaining 45% stake) to diversify the education bet toward outcome-linked upskilling rather than pure content/discovery.

The AI & Deeptech Bet — Built Before It Was Consensus

Info Edge deployed over ₹1,000 Cr across 54+ AI-native and deeptech companies between 2020 and March 2026 — spanning enterprise AI, robotics, semiconductors, space tech, biotech and electric mobility — well before either theme became a global consensus trade. Fifteen of the 28 AI-native companies have already secured follow-on institutional funding rounds, and select deeptech names (ePlane, Manastu Space) have won government RDI-scheme allocations contingent on private co-investment. Returns here (1.8x combined) are naturally far behind the mature consumer-tech sleeve (13.5x), reflecting a much younger book.

Investment Philosophy & Structure

Info Edge runs its venture activity through a 50:50 joint-venture structure with Temasek's MacRitchie Investments (Info Edge Venture Fund, Info Edge Capital, Capital 2B, Karkardooma Trust), evaluating a few hundred companies per quarter and investing in only 3-4. Founder Sanjeev Bikhchandani's shareholder letter emphasises disciplined entry pricing — "expensive early rounds almost always hinder a company's ability to raise capital during difficult periods" — citing the 2008 Policybazaar and 2010 Zomato entries as proof of the philosophy. IPO remains the stated preferred exit route.

The Sum-of-the-Parts Framing

A meaningful share of Info Edge's ~₹75,000 Cr market capitalisation is arguably explained by its ~₹41,300 Cr investment portfolio (dominated by Eternal and PB Fintech), leaving the core operating businesses — Naukri, 99acres, Jeevansathi, Shiksha — to be valued on the residual. Some analysts have argued Info Edge would be more cleanly valued if the investment book were housed in a separately listed entity, since investors seeking pure-play Naukri exposure are currently forced to also carry venture-style, illiquid, binary-outcome risk from the AI/deeptech sleeve.

🏛️ The Examined Business
Info Edge's Q4 FY26 results tell a genuinely encouraging operating story hiding underneath a genuinely confusing headline P&L. Strip away the ₹5,535.7 Cr exceptional-gain-inflated standalone PAT and the Eternal/PB Fintech-driven ₹6,217.2 Cr consolidated comprehensive loss, and what remains is a recruitment business firing on all cylinders (58% operating margin, AI products scaling fast), a real estate business that just turned profitable after a decade of patient investment, a matrimony/dating portfolio nearing breakeven, and one clearly troubled segment — Shiksha — that is the company's own early warning signal for how AI-driven search behaviour can hollow out a discovery-classifieds model from the demand side, not the supply side. The Q1 FY27 billings update (released ahead of full results, expected around early August 2026) suggests this operating momentum has continued into the new fiscal year, with Naukri and 99acres both accelerating even as Shiksha's decline deepens. The bigger long-term question for Info Edge shareholders may not be about Naukri at all — it's whether the same AI-search dynamic hurting Shiksha today eventually reaches recruitment and real estate discovery too, and whether the ~₹41,300 Cr investment portfolio (still dominated by two ageing, mature bets) can find its next Zomato or Policybazaar before that question needs answering.
