📚 Metric Glossary — Insurance vs Manufacturing: GWP / GDPI (Gross Written / Direct Premium): total premium collected — equivalent to revenue in manufacturing. APE (Annualised Premium Equivalent): regular premium + 10% of single premium — normalises new business comparison. VNB (Value of New Business): present value of profits from new policies — the primary profitability metric for life insurers. VNB Margin: VNB ÷ APE — like gross margin in manufacturing. Higher = better quality business. Embedded Value (EV / IEV): adjusted net worth + present value of in-force policies — captures future locked-in profits. Combined Ratio (health/general): (Claims + Expenses) ÷ Net Premium — below 100% = underwriting profit; above 100% = underwriting loss. Loss Ratio: Claims incurred ÷ Net premium earned. CISR: IFRS-equivalent of Combined Ratio (used by Niva Bupa). Solvency Ratio: Available capital ÷ Required capital — IRDAI minimum 150%. Persistency Ratio: % of policies renewed at 13th / 61st month. RoEV: Return on Embedded Value — capital efficiency metric for life insurers. TPA: Third-Party Administrator — earns service fees for processing claims; NOT an insurer.

🛡 Life Insurers — Investment Framework
⚖ IRDAI Regulatory Framework — Investment Regulations for Life Insurers
Life insurers follow IRDAI (Investment) Regulations, 2016 and the Master Circular on Investments 2024. Key mandatory allocation rules: (1) Minimum 25% in Central Government Securities (CGS); (2) Minimum 50% in approved securities (Central + State Govt bonds); (3) Not more than 15% in equity for non-ULIP Par/Non-Par funds; (4) Not more than 5% in any single corporate bond; (5) Policyholders' funds and shareholders' funds managed separately; (6) ULIP funds follow policyholder-chosen mandates (equity/debt/balanced). Life insurer investments split into: Participating (PAR) fund, Non-Participating fund, ULIP fund, and Shareholders' fund. Investment yield on PAR fund directly determines policyholder bonus. Solvency = Available Solvency Margin ÷ Required Solvency Margin (min 150%).

🏥 Health Insurers — Investment Framework
⚖ IRDAI Regulatory Framework — Investment Regulations for Health / General Insurers
Standalone Health Insurers (SAHIs) follow IRDAI (Investment) Regulations, 2016 with different rules from life insurers. Key rules: (1) Minimum 30% in Central and State Government securities; (2) Minimum 55% in approved securities; (3) Maximum 20% in equity/equity-linked; (4) Minimum 5% in Infrastructure and Social Sector. Unlike life insurers, SAHIs do NOT hold long-duration ALM-matched portfolios — investment horizons shorter (claims payable in 1-2 years). Investment income supplements underwriting income and is critical when Combined Ratio exceeds 100%. Key difference from life: No surrender value, no embedded value concept — pure underwriting + investment income business. IRDAI Master Circular 2024 added ESG considerations and improved reporting norms for all insurers.
