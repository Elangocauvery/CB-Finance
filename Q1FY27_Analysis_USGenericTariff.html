What's happening (as of the real 22 Jul 2026 policy), who is at risk, how big the opportunity is, and India's response

Source: Association for Accessible Medicines (AAM) / IQVIA Institute, 2025 U.S. Generic & Biosimilar Medicines Savings Report (2024 data). Exact splits move a point or two year to year (recent AAM releases show generics at 12-13% of spend); India's ~40%-by-volume share of the US generics market therefore sits inside a large-volume, low-dollar-value segment — which is also why a 100-200% tariff on this segment (Track 2) would hit unit economics hard even though it touches a small share of total US drug spending.

⏱️ "IMMEDIATE" IMPACT — there isn't one, yet

✓
No near-term price/supply shock. 0% tariff holds through Jul 2028 — the original "higher costs now" framing is wrong for the next two years.
!
Real impact shifts to 2028–29. Pressure builds only if exporters haven't onshored or diversified by then.
⚠
Capital-allocation impact is immediate. Boards must decide now on US plants / M&A / diversification to be ready before Aug 2028.

✓

✓

OPPORTUNITY

The 2-year zero-tariff window is itself an opportunity: it gives Indian companies time to build US manufacturing, deepen CDMO contracts (exempt from this policy), and grow India-domestic and non-US export markets — diversifying away from US-generic concentration before any tariff actually bites in 2028.

  • Reduce US concentration risk over time

✅ KEY TAKEAWAYS

✔The US generic tariff is a 2028+ headwind, not a near-term one — 0% holds through Jul 2028.
✔Companies with the highest US-generics exposure and no onshoring plan (Aurobindo, Zydus, Natco) carry the most 2028+ risk.
✔CDMO/API players and India-domestic branded players are structurally the least exposed — a genuinely different risk class from finished-generic exporters.
✔India's generic exports remain critical to US and global healthcare affordability — the underlying demand case is unaffected by this policy.
✔Engagement, US onshoring, value-upgradation and market diversification — inside the 2-year window — are the real levers, not near-term price pass-through.

🎯 THE BIG PICTURE

2026-2028: A rare grace period — margins and growth continue largely undisturbed by this specific policy.

2028 onward: Real bifurcation — companies that used the window to onshore/diversify keep US access; those that didn't face a 100-200% tariff wall.
